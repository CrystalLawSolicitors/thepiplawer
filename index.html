Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

What is PIP?

Personal Independence Payment (PIP) is a UK benefit to help with the extra costs of living with a long-term health condition or disability. It is not means-tested and can be paid whether or not you are in work.

PIP is made up of two parts:

  • Daily living component – help with everyday activities
  • Mobility component – help with getting around

Awards are based on how your condition affects you, not just your diagnosis. TPL helps you understand and explain this clearly.

Key facts
  • PIP is assessed by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP).
  • It can be paid on top of other benefits and earnings.
  • You can challenge decisions through reconsiderations and appeals.

For official GOV.UK guidance and current rates, always refer to the latest government information. This website provides supporting guidance and does not replace official advice.

Steps & Criteria

Eligibility, claiming and appeals – step by step

1. Check basic eligibility

  • Age requirements (usually 16+ and under State Pension age for new claims).
  • Condition or disability expected to last at least 12 months.
  • You have difficulties with daily living and/or mobility.

We help you map your difficulties to the PIP activities and “descriptors” used in the assessment.

2. Start a new PIP claim

  • Initial phone call to start the claim.
  • PIP2 “How your disability affects you” form.
  • Gathering medical and supporting evidence.

Use our checklists and guides to answer clearly and avoid common mistakes.

3. Assessments & decisions

  • Paper-based or telephone / face-to-face assessment.
  • DWP decision letter with your score and award.

If you disagree with the outcome, we explain your options and time limits.

4. Mandatory reconsiderations

If you think the decision is wrong, you usually must ask for a mandatory reconsideration first.

  • Explain why you believe the decision is wrong.
  • Correct misunderstandings and add missing evidence.

TPL can draft reconsideration letters and help structure your reasons.

5. Appeals to the Tribunal

If the reconsideration doesn’t resolve things, you can appeal to an independent tribunal.

  • Appeal forms and deadlines.
  • Preparing a clear written statement.
  • What to expect at a hearing.

Advocacy and representative support may be available through TPL and our legal partners.

6. Ongoing awards & reviews

PIP awards are often time-limited and reviewed. We help you prepare for:

  • Award reviews and “light touch” reviews.
  • Changes of circumstances.
  • What to do if your condition worsens or improves.

FAQs

Common questions about PIP

Do I need a solicitor to claim PIP?

No. Many people claim PIP without a solicitor. However, advice and representation can be helpful if your case is complex, you have been refused, or you are going through reconsideration or appeal.

Will using TPL affect my PIP decision?

No. DWP does not treat your claim differently because you have used a guidance website. Our aim is to help you present your situation as clearly and accurately as possible using the existing rules.

Is this the official government website?

No. TPL – The PIP Lawyers is not part of the government or DWP. We are a guidance and support service, with access to solicitor-level advice via Crystal Law Solicitors where needed. For official information always refer to GOV.UK.
